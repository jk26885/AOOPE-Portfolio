Week 5

Tasks Done

  • Studied the Strategy pattern as a way of defining a family of interchangeable algorithms, encapsulating each one so they can be swapped independently of the client that uses them.
  • Implemented a pricing engine that holds a reference to whichever pricing strategy is currently active and delegates every calculation to it, rather than branching on pricing logic internally.
  • Built four interchangeable strategies: a standard tiered strategy, a demand-based strategy, an ML-informed strategy, and a bundle-discount strategy; all satisfying the same pricing interface.
  • Wrote a test that injects a mock strategy to verify the engine delegates correctly, independent of any specific algorithm's actual output.
Reflection

Week 5 Reflection

Unit 5 introduced me to behavioural design patterns and helped me understand how objects interact and communicate within a software system. Through the lecturecast and reading, I learned how patterns such as Strategy, Observer, Chain of Responsibility, Template Method, Command, and State provide structured approaches to managing object behaviour while improving flexibility and maintainability.

A key learning point from this unit was recognising that effective software design is not only about creating and structuring objects but also about managing how those objects collaborate. The Strategy pattern was particularly interesting because it demonstrated how algorithms can be encapsulated and exchanged without modifying the code that uses them. This highlighted the benefits of loose coupling and reinforced principles studied in unit 2, such as abstraction and the Open/Closed Principle.

I also found the Observer pattern highly relevant due to its application in cybersecurity and monitoring systems. As someone working in IT infrastructure and security, I could relate the concept of notifying dependent objects when events occur to real-world monitoring, alerting, and incident management solutions. This helped me bridge the gap between theoretical design patterns and practical enterprise systems

Outcome

Unit Outcome

Overall, Unit 5 expanded my understanding of how design patterns can be used to manage interactions between objects effectively. The knowledge gained strengthened my software design skills and provided a foundation for later topics such as software architecture, concurrency, and secure coding
