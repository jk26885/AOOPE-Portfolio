Week 1

Tasks Done

  • Revisited the four pillars of OOP — Inheritance, Polymorphism, Abstraction, and Encapsulation — and their role in software design.
  • Reviewed classes, objects, constructors, destructors, and access control mechanisms (public, private, protected).
  • Explored how OOP principles apply to large-scale and secure software design.
Reflection

Week 1 Reflection

Unit 1 reinforced the importance of the fundamental principles of Object-Oriented Programming, including classes, objects, inheritance, encapsulation, abstraction, and polymorphism. Through practical exercises and subsequent artefacts, I developed a stronger understanding of how these concepts supported the creation of modular, reusable, and maintainable software systems

The unit also highlighted the role of OOP in large-scale and secure software design. Understanding concepts such as encapsulation, separation of concerns, and layered design provide an important foundation for the more advanced topics to be covered later in the module.

Outcome

By working through these introductory exercises, I was able to refresh myself on OOP basics
